# Researcher Review Protocol

## Purpose

This protocol defines how researchers review provisional interpretations generated by the assessment framework.

## Principle

The system proposes.
The researcher decides.

## Required Review Inputs

Before confirming any Domain Understanding or AI-CFT interpretation, the researcher must be able to inspect:

- supporting evidence
- contradictory evidence
- confidence
- uncertainty
- traceability
- alternative explanations
- identified misconceptions
- evidence sufficiency indicators

## Minimum Review Sequence

1. Inspect the provisional claim.
2. Review the linked Domain Understanding dimensions.
3. Inspect the contributing Learning Objects.
4. Inspect the supporting observable behaviours.
5. Verify the underlying evidence units in their source context.
6. Review contradictory evidence and unresolved ambiguities.
7. Check confidence and sufficiency indicators.
8. Confirm, revise, defer, or reject the recommendation.
9. Record a justification.

## Allowed Researcher Decisions

- confirm
- confirm with qualification
- revise upward
- revise downward
- defer pending more evidence
- reject

## Required Decision Record

Each researcher decision must record:

- reviewer id
- date
- claim reviewed
- decision outcome
- rationale
- evidence consulted
- unresolved concerns

## Reliability Safeguards

- use shared review guidance
- calibrate reviewers on sampled cases
- flag low-confidence cases for mandatory review
- flag contradiction-heavy cases for extended review

## Validity Safeguards

- do not accept machine recommendations without trace inspection
- do not resolve contradictions silently
- do not infer competency from a single task or source

## Escalation Cases

Mandatory escalation is required when:

- evidence is sparse
- evidence is contradictory
- extraction confidence is low
- behaviour interpretation is ambiguous
- AI-CFT interpretation exceeds domain evidence strength

## Auditability

All final researcher decisions must be stored in a way that preserves:

- the original recommendation
- the final decision
- the rationale for any change
